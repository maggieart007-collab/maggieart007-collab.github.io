Problem

Companies are quietly paying a burnout tax: high turnover, declining productivity, and rising sick leaves. Teams lose focus, projects slow down, and the business bleeds money —

According to research, employee burnout can cost a company $4,000 to $21,000 per employee per year. For a company with 1,000 employees, this amounts to approximately $5 million in annual losses.

At the same time, existing wellness tools fail. They live outside the workflow and feel like another task to complete in an already overloaded day.

Hybrid teams add another layer of friction:

  • office employees feel drained by forced team-building;
  • remote employees feel excluded and disconnected from informal networks.

The result is two cultures inside one company — growing toxicity, lower eNPS, and stalled innovation.

According to Viwell, burnout is associated not only with turnover but also with increased healthcare costs: approximately $125–190 billion annually are linked to employee mental health issues.

Why it matters

Low engagement directly translates into losses:

  • higher churn among mid-level specialists,
  • loss of expertise,
  • delayed releases,
  • drop in service quality.

The problem isn't that people don't want to engage.
The problem is that engagement today requires effort — and effort is the one resource burned-out teams no longer have.

Also, according to Gallup and other studies (via RBC review), up to 48% of employees report that they might leave a company due to burnout.
88% of employees who feel that their company cares about their wellbeing are more engaged and motivated to contribute.

Why Lumi

LUMI integrates rest and light social interaction into daily tools — not on top of them.
It creates natural, low-effort micro-moments of connection and recovery, without forcing participation.

Mechanics like Stealth Mentor and Interest-based Coffee Breaks turn engagement into something effortless, personal, and psychologically safe.

LUMI replaces artificial team-building with small, meaningful actions that rebuild trust, energy, and belonging.

How interaction works in LUMI

LUMI encourages people to take small moments for themselves every day — to build emotional resilience long before stress reaches a critical point.

But how do we motivate users to actually want to engage?

The system is built on positive reinforcement and the growth of a personal companion.
Every completed activity rewards the user with coins that can be spent in the in-app store on physical merchandise, cinema tickets, or cosmetic upgrades for their character.
This gentle reward cycle helps form a new, healthy habit: taking care of yourself.

LUMI brings together a variety of practices so that each person can choose what feels right for them — from relaxation and mindfulness to light movement or emotional balancing.
All profiles remain anonymous, ensuring that communication and social interactions inside the app feel safe, comfortable, and pressure-free.

Rewards that support healthy habits

LUMI transforms small daily actions into meaningful motivation.

By completing breathing practices, eye exercises or short meditations, users earn coins that can be exchanged for real rewards: branded merchandise, small gifts, cinema tickets, or exclusive outfits for their character.

This system of positive reinforcement helps people build sustainable self-care habits — one small step at a time.

Seamless workplace integration

LUMI gently supports users during their workday without interrupting their flow. Before an important meeting, the system can send a short, unobtrusive reminder: take a quick breathing practice to reduce stress and enter the conversation with a clearer mind.

This feature takes less than a minute but helps build long-term emotional resilience and creates a healthier work environment. Focus Mode helps you stay fully immersed in the task at hand by gently reducing digital noise.

When activated, it temporarily silences non-essential notifications, dims background distractions, and launches a simple built-in timer to structure your work into comfortable intervals.

Previous Concepts & Early Explorations

The project began with a simple idea: helping employees take small, meaningful breaks during the workday — moments that reduce stress, refresh focus, and bring a bit of variety into the routine.

My early explorations were centered around simplicity. The first concept featured a humorous parrot character and a minimal visual style built on black line art. The goal was to create something intentionally light, approachable, and distraction-free. However, as the idea evolved, it became clear that the project needed a richer emotional component and a more engaging interaction model.

The next direction was inspired by tabletop card games — a playful system with structured mechanics and collectible elements. Although visually strong, this approach had a high entry barrier and required too much cognitive effort for a product designed to help people relax rather than concentrate even more.

As the concept continued to develop, the focus shifted toward creating an intuitive, soothing environment with clear navigation and gentle interactions. This led to the final idea: a character inspired by digital pets, something users could care for and gradually grow alongside. This emotional connection supports long-term engagement while keeping the interface clean, warm, and simple to use.
